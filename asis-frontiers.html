What the census cannot pin, in bits

The atom census fixes the aggregate composition exactly. But it is a coarse invariant: it does not determine which five monomers, nor their handedness. Reading those requires finer channels — and one of them has no channel at all. The bar shows, per layer, how many bits the census leaves undetermined, and whether the message can carry them.

C · decodability phase transition

Blind decodability, with finite-size scaling

Is an anti-cryptographic message always blindly decodable? Synthetic targets of increasing structural complexity were encoded (run backward a fixed depth) and tested: a target is decodable if a blind compression clock has an isolated minimum at the true depth. Recovery is sharp in complexity — never certain at complexity one, always by complexity five — reproducing the conjecture the paper states but does not scale.

P(blindly decodable) vs target complexity, for grid sizes 32/48/64 (40 trials each, depth 40). The threshold near complexity 2 sharpens with size — the finite-size signature the paper flagged as future work.

extra · persistent homology (H₀)

Eleven components, stable across scale

Growing a linking radius over the 625-pixel cloud, the number of connected components collapses and then holds at a wide plateau of exactly 11 — the five molecules plus the six assembly points — across radii of ~3–16 px. Scale-invariance is a topological fingerprint of the decode, independent of the size code.

extra · condensation energetics

The water paradox, made numerical

Assembling the kit into the pentapeptide releases four waters. A gas-phase MMFF94 estimate gives – kcal for kit → peptide + 4 H₂O. But in a prebiotic ocean the same condensation runs uphill — you would be shedding water into water — which is exactly why the message sends the unassembled kit and the combining rule, not a finished chain. The six points prescribe the operation the chemistry itself makes costly in bulk water.
